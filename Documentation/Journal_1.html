Week 1 documentation

Usability testing as a thing

By far the most important and interesting thing that I learned is that you need to do usability testing. I'm used to more of a functional testing, does the program produce the expected outputs based on a specific set of inputs. But I can definitely see the value in usability testing. All the work that you put in to making sure that your website has the correct features and that they work as intended would go to waste if the users can't use the site correctly. And while not every user is the same, as a developer, we know how the system should work, where things are and how to use them. But that doesn't mean that a user that has never seen our website will. And if they can't use it, then you won't have any traffic, making your site useless.

Testing often

Testing often is a concept that I am already familiar with from my programming experience. The idea that even if you only have one person to test your site, and that your better off doing more rounds of testing with fewer people then less rounds with more people makes a lot of sense to me. While more of a functional level of testing (making sure it produces the correct outputs), I have on many occasions found errors in my programs on the second or third round of testing that you miss on the first round, either because you were stuck on other problem, or they weren't apparent at first. And you want to test to make sure that the changes that you made are actually going to fix the problem, and that they haven't created additional problems.

For my Project

It's clear that I'm going to have to do regular usability testing to ensure that people are going to be able to navigate and use my site. Even if the test is as simple as asking someone if they can find a particular page, getting others input on your work helps to make sure that your end up with a high quality product. As a developer, I'm likely to become blinded to issues from the simple fact that I have spent so much time working on the site, that I might not see that decisions that I have made don't make sense, or are confusing to the user.
